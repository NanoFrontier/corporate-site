Tohoku University startup NanoFrontier raises approximately 150 million yen in funding

2025-11-10

NanoFrontier raises approximately 150 million yen through third-party allotment

NanoFrontier Co., Ltd. (Headquarters: Sendai City, Miyagi Prefecture; Representative Director: Seiya Inoue) completed a funding round of approximately 150 million yen through third-party allotment in October 2025.

The investors in this round include three venture capital firms and one corporate investor:

  • Genesia Ventures, Inc.
  • Animal Spirits LLC
  • Mobile Internet Capital, Inc.
  • Nitto Boseki Co., Ltd.

Additionally, the company has secured over 100 million yen in grants, including support from the Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry and the Small and Medium Enterprise Agency’s R&D Support Program for Growth-Oriented SMEs (Go-Tech Program).

Use of funds

The raised funds will be primarily used for the following purposes:

  1. Establishing a research and development base within Tohoku University
  2. Accelerating PoC for organic nano-dye reagents capable of immediate on-site colorimetric detection of specific harmful substances such as certain PFAS
  3. Developing continuous mass production equipment for nanoparticles
  4. Setting up an automated research and development pipeline
  5. Strengthening recruitment (in synthetic chemistry, analysis and quality assurance, data engineering, and operations)

In particular, the company will focus on real-time detection of specific harmful substances such as certain PFAS, advancing the development of technology that instantly visualizes interactions with targets as optical responses using organic dye nanoparticles.

CEO & CTO Profiles

Co-founder & CEO: Seiya Inoue

Graduated from the University of Tokyo’s Faculty of Agriculture and completed a master’s degree (organic compound synthesis, machine learning research). Gained experience in AI development, real estate funds, and VC while in school, then joined Microsoft to engage in Azure-based development support and technical sales. Later founded a company developing AI applications. In April 2025, co-founded NanoFrontier to develop and commercialize functional organic materials using nanoparticle technology from Tohoku University.

Co-founder & CTO: Hiroki Oka, Ph.D.

Associate Professor at the Institute of Multidisciplinary Research for Advanced Materials, Tohoku University. Focusing on unique functional organic materials, he works on developing hydrophilic amine polymers, researching electrolytes for next-generation batteries, pioneering organic solid catalysts, and developing electrode active materials for environmentally friendly batteries. He also focuses on hydrogen production and storage technology using biomass. Received numerous awards including the 11th JSPS IKUSHI Prize (2021), the 12th New Chemical Technology Research Encouragement Award (2023), and the 47th Encouragement Award from the Adhesion Society of Japan (2025). In April 2025, co-founded NanoFrontier to develop and commercialize functional organic materials using nanoparticle technology from Tohoku University.
